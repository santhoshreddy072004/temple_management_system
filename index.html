Featured Temples

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism. Built in the 16th century, its upper floors are covered with 750 kg of pure gold. The temple is renowned for its langar, serving free meals to over 100,000 visitors daily. The sacred Amrit Sarovar surrounding it is believed to have healing properties.

Meenakshi Temple

The Meenakshi Amman Temple in Madurai is a historic marvel dating back to the 6th century BC. Its 14 magnificent gopurams (gateway towers) are adorned with over 33,000 sculptures. The temple complex spans 14 acres and features the famous Hall of Thousand Pillars, each carved with unique mythological scenes.

Kedarnath Temple

Perched at 11,755 feet in the Himalayas, Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Built in the 8th century, this temple remarkably survived the 2013 floods. The temple is accessible only six months a year due to extreme weather and is surrounded by breathtaking snow-capped peaks.

Tirupati Balaji Temple

Sri Venkateswara Temple at Tirupati is the world's richest temple, receiving offerings worth millions daily. Located on the seven peaks of Tirumala Hills, it's visited by 50,000-100,000 pilgrims daily. The temple's kitchen is one of the largest in the world, serving free meals to thousands of devotees.

Somnath Temple

The Somnath Temple in Gujarat is the first among the twelve Jyotirlingas. Rebuilt several times after repeated invasions, the current structure (1951) represents the resilience of Indian culture. The temple's shikhara (spire) is 15 meters high and adorned with an 8.2-meter flag pole.

Jagannath Temple

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is famous for its annual Rath Yatra (chariot festival). Built in the 12th century, it's known for its unique architecture where the flag atop the temple dome appears to flutter in the opposite direction of the wind. The temple kitchen can cook for 100,000 people daily using traditional methods.
